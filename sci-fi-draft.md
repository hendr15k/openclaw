# Sci-Fi Book Recommendations for Hendrik

Based on your background in Elektrotechnik at FH Aachen, I've curated a selection of hard science fiction books that emphasize scientific accuracy, technical detail, and plausible engineering concepts—perfect for someone with your technical interests.

## Top Recommendations

### 1. *The Martian* by Andy Weir (2011)
**Why it fits:** This novel is essentially a survival story driven by engineering problem-solving. Astronaut Mark Watney uses his expertise in botany, mechanics, and chemistry to overcome one obstacle after another on Mars. The book reads like a series of technical challenges and solutions, making it ideal for an engineering mind. You'll appreciate the detailed calculations, orbital mechanics, and realistic spaceflight procedures.

### 2. *Project Hail Mary* by Andy Weir (2021)
**Why it fits:** From the same author, this book features even more rigorous scientific exploration. The protagonist, Ryland Grace, must save Earth from an astrophysical threat using biology, chemistry, and physics. The story includes detailed scientific explanations that are integral to the plot, with a strong emphasis on collaborative problem-solving across species—a great fit for your technical curiosity.

### 3. *Leviathan Wakes* (The Expanse #1) by James S.A. Corey (2011)
**Why it fits:** While more space opera than pure hard sci-fi, this series excels in its realistic portrayal of space travel, physics, and engineering challenges. The Epstein drive, orbital mechanics, and life support systems are depicted with plausible technical detail. The political intrigue adds depth, but the core science remains solid—ideal for someone who enjoys both technical accuracy and compelling narrative.

### 4. *Seveneves* by Neal Stephenson (2015)
**Why it fits:** This novel is a masterclass in hard science fiction. Stephenson explores orbital mechanics, genetics, and space engineering in exquisite detail. The first two-thirds of the book focus on the technical survival of humanity after a catastrophic event, with extensive explanations of orbital dynamics, spacecraft design, and genetic engineering—perfect for deep technical engagement.

### 5. *The Three-Body Problem* by Liu Cixin (2008, English translation 2014)
**Why it fits:** This Hugo-winning novel combines astrophysics, nanotechnology, and computer science with a gripping narrative. The Trisolaran environment and the sophon technology are explained with scientific rigor. The Cultural Revolution backdrop adds historical depth, but the hard science elements—particularly the nanotech and quantum concepts—will appeal to your technical side.

### 6. *Children of Time* by Adrian Tchaikovsky (2015)
**Why it fits:** This award-winning novel explores speculative biology and genetics with impressive detail. The story follows the evolution of spiders on a terraformed planet, incorporating real principles of genetics, adaptation, and symbiosis. The hard science elements are woven into a rich narrative about intelligence and evolution—great for someone interested in the intersection of biology and technology.

## How to Choose
- For pure engineering problem-solving: Start with *The Martian* or *Project Hail Mary*.
- For orbital mechanics and space engineering: *Seveneves* or *Leviathan Wakes*.
- For cutting-edge physics and nanotech: *The Three-Body Problem*.
- For evolutionary biology and genetics: *Children of Time*.

All of these books balance technical accuracy with engaging storytelling, ensuring you'll learn something new while being thoroughly entertained. Given your Elektrotechnik background, you'll likely appreciate the attention to detail in the engineering and scientific aspects of these novels.
